# Hospital_Perception_Summary

**Objective:** 
This project aimed to investigate how patient trust in U.S. hospitals changed before and during the COVID-19 pandemic. 
In particular, I will attempt to answer the following questions
1. Have hospitals' survey scores improved over the past 9 years?
2. Are there any specific areas where hospitals have made more progress than others?
3. Are there any major areas of opportunity remaining?
4. What recommendations can you make to hospitals to help them further improve the patient experience?

**Metrics:**
The primary metric used for this assessment was the Net Promoter Score (NPS), a widely recognized indicator of consumer loyalty and trust across various industries.

**Data Source:** 
The data was obtained from the HCAHPS survey, which assesses patient perspectives on hospital care from the year 2015 to 2023.

**Thought Process**
Why NPS?
NPS is the difference between the top percentage scores (Promoters) and the bottom percentage scores (Detractors), hence it considers the opinions of both Advocators and Opposers. Basically, NPS is used to measure the overall satisfaction of members towards hospital care.

Why focus on the pandemic?
COVID-19 brought about challenges to healthcare sector, especially hospitals where new safety protocols and social distancing measures were introduced. Furthermore, due to doctors, nurses and staff’s increased workload that came with the uptick in patient admission, communication and responsiveness were compromised. These factors affect patients’ perception towards hospital care negatively. Therefore, the purpose of this project is to understand areas that experienced the biggest decline and come up with plans to address those issues to regain patient trust.  

How is the project structured?
The project includes two main parts, namely Executive Summary and State Analysis. The Executive Summary part contains most salient findings from the data, including NPS trend and NPS performance by measure and by state. It concludes with key recommendations on how to tackle those weaknesses. The State Analysis part drills into the current state of Texas hospitals in terms of patient satisfaction. It analysis  key drivers that contribute to their success as well as areas for improvement to boost their NPS score.
Each part starts from high level and goes into depth based on findings from the preceding sections

A-	PART 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
1.	Section 1: The pandemic's impact: A shift in patient satisfaction indicators
2.	Section 2: Identify key drivers shaping patient trust
3.	Section 3: Understand key states to monitor- Identify Drifters and Thrivers
4.	Section 4: Roadmap to rebuilding patient trust

B-	PART 2: STATE ANALYSIS
I will be focusing on Texas for this detailed analysis because the state displays resilience growth during the pandemic, they have rooms for improvement.
1.	Section 1: Texas’s decent position in patient satisfaction
2.	Section 2: Identify key drivers that contribute to the performance of Texas in patient satisfaction
3.	Section 3: Identify areas that Texas hospitals still can work on to improve their NPS further
4.	Section 4: Texas hospitals’ action plans for winning at patient satisfaction.
